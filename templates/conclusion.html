Our Viewpoints

As we hypothesized, CCTV cameras in Baltimore are not evenly distributed across the city in terms of geography or population size.

Nor can we observe any consistent relationship between the presence of cameras and the volume or types of crimes in a given area.

However, there does see to be a strong relationship between the numbers of cameras in an area and demographic factors like income and race. Areas that are poorer and less white are more likely to be more heavily surveilled.

Challenges & Further Considerations

One challenge we faced in this project was reconciling various geographic reference points across our datasets. Some datasets tracked by “neighborhood”, while others were categorized by latitude and longitude and/or zip code. This made is difficult to always precisely compare data like crime records and demographics, for example.

In order to fairly measure any impact that CCTV cameras have on crime in Baltimore, we would need to introduce a time series component. Ideally, we would track crime rates in certain neighborhoods before and after cameras were installed, holding for other variables. While we had access to extensive historical crime data, the BPD does not provide data on when individual cameras were installed.
