Insights From This Visualization

  • There are over 200 neighborhoods in Baltimore, each with widely varying numbers of crimes per year. Among even just the top five ranked by number of crimes, the difference between the first and fifth ranked neighborhoods is nearly two-fold.
  • The most common types of crimes committed varies by neighborhood as well. In Downtown, nearly half (47%) of the incidents are “crimes against the person”, whereas that same crime type comprises only 19% of incidents in Canton.
  • There is no clear correlation between number of crimes in a neighborhood and number of surveillance cameras. Downtown has both the highest number of crimes and cameras. However, the neighborhood with the second highest number of crimes, Frankford, had over 1,000 crimes, but only one camera.
